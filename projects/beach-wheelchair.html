Motorized Beach Wheelchair

Accessibility Design • 2024 • Avalon Beach Patrol, NJ

Overview

This project enhanced beach accessibility through a cost-effective motorized wheelchair design. Working with Avalon Beach Patrol in New Jersey, I transformed their manual wheelchair model into a motorized version that improved user independence while maintaining affordability at a $1,500 budget target.

Traditional beach wheelchairs require constant caregiver assistance for navigation through sand. This motorized solution empowers users with independent joystick control, significantly improving the beach experience for individuals with mobility challenges.

Design Process

Initial Research & Planning

I began by examining existing manual beach wheelchair limitations and identifying key challenges:

  • Difficulty navigating through sand requiring significant caregiver effort
  • Complete dependence on caregivers limiting user autonomy
  • High cost of commercial motorized alternatives ($3,000+)
  • Need for waterproof components in beach environment

CAD Design Phase

I fully modeled the wheelchair in Fusion 360, focusing on:

  • Custom motor and battery mounting systems
  • Optimized weight distribution for sand navigation
  • Waterproofing integration throughout the design
  • Assembly instructions for straightforward construction

Build & Construction

The construction phase involved careful material selection and assembly:

  • Frame: PVC construction with marine-grade materials and reinforced joints
  • Motors: Dual waterproof electric motors for redundancy and power
  • Power System: 12V 50Ah LiFePO4 batteries for extended runtime
  • Control: Intuitive joystick system for easy user operation
  • Safety: Waterproof electronics enclosure, safety belt, and water sensors

Results & Impact

The motorized beach wheelchair successfully addressed all design goals:

Maneuverability Rating 9.0/10

vs 2.0/10 traditional

Total Cost $1,500

vs $3,000+ commercial

Key Improvements

  • Independent Control: Joystick operation eliminates need for constant caregiver pushing
  • Enhanced Stability: Improved weight distribution and wide wheelbase for sand terrain
  • Waterproof Design: Marine-grade materials and sealed electronics for beach environment
  • Cost-Effective: 50% cost reduction compared to commercial alternatives
  • Sustainable: LiFePO4 batteries provide long lifespan and reliable performance

Technical Skills Used

  • CAD modeling and assembly design (Fusion 360)
  • Electromechanical system integration
  • Material selection for marine environments
  • User-centered design and accessibility considerations
  • Waterproofing and environmental protection design
  • Cost optimization and design for manufacturing

Learnings

This project taught me the importance of designing for real-world constraints. Working within a strict $1,500 budget while meeting waterproofing and performance requirements required creative problem-solving and careful material selection.

Most importantly, I learned that impactful engineering isn't always about the most advanced technology—it's about understanding user needs and creating practical solutions that genuinely improve quality of life. The Avalon Beach Patrol's continued use of the wheelchair validates that accessible design can be both affordable and effective.
