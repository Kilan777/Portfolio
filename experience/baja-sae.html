Baja SAE Racing

Chassis Lead • Freshman Year - Present • Olin College

Overview

Baja SAE is an intercollegiate engineering design competition where students design, build, and race off-road vehicles that can withstand rough terrain. Teams must engineer vehicles to survive severe conditions while following strict safety requirements, competing in events including acceleration, maneuverability, hill climb, and endurance racing.

I was promoted to Chassis Lead within my first semester as a freshman, taking charge of frame design and coordinating with all subteams to ensure successful integration of components across the vehicle.

Technical Leadership

  • Managed chassis design and manufacturing processes from concept to completion
  • Coordinated component integration across all engineering subteams
  • Led design reviews and validation meetings to ensure design integrity
  • Ensured manufacturing feasibility and adherence to competition requirements

Fabrication Process

Our fabrication process required precision and careful planning. Each tube was checked against CAD specifications upon arrival. We created custom jigs using a CNC router and 1/8-inch plywood to support tubes during welding, with main tubes supported in a minimum of 2 locations. Both TIG and MIG welding techniques were applied depending on the structural requirements.

Key Design Improvements

  • Cockpit Design: Increased interior space and improved driver ergonomics for better performance during long endurance races
  • Engine Integration: Redesigned rear frame to allow side engine removal, significantly reducing maintenance time
  • Suspension Integration: Widened footbox and optimized mounting points for improved handling and driver comfort

Technical Skills & Tools

Throughout this project, I developed and applied a comprehensive set of engineering skills:

  • CAD modeling using SolidWorks Weldments feature for tubular frame design
  • Finite Element Analysis (FEA) for structural validation and optimization
  • Advanced welding expertise with both TIG and MIG techniques
  • Design for Manufacturing (DFM) principles to ensure buildability
  • Cross-functional team coordination and technical communication

Impact & Learnings

Leading the chassis team as a freshman taught me the importance of cross-functional collaboration and design thinking. I learned that successful engineering isn't just about creating the strongest or lightest design—it's about balancing multiple constraints including manufacturability, cost, safety, and performance. The experience of coordinating with suspension, powertrain, and ergonomics teams showed me how critical communication is in complex engineering projects.
