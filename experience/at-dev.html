AT Dev Inc

Robotics Engineering Intern • Summer 2023

Overview

AT Dev Inc specializes in developing assistive technology for individuals with physical disabilities. As a Robotics Engineering Intern, I contributed to the design and prototyping of innovative assistive devices that improve quality of life for users with mobility challenges.

Key Projects & Responsibilities

  • Developed and prototyped assistive technology devices for individuals with physical disabilities
  • Designed leg brace with innovative fastening system using CAD software
  • Applied hands-on machining techniques to fabricate prototypes and functional components
  • Collaborated with occupational therapists to understand user needs and design requirements
  • Conducted testing and iteration based on user feedback and clinical requirements

Innovative Leg Brace Design

One of my primary projects involved designing a leg brace with a novel fastening system. This project required:

  • Understanding biomechanical constraints and user comfort requirements
  • CAD modeling to ensure proper fit and function
  • Selecting appropriate materials balancing strength, weight, and comfort
  • Hands-on machining to create functional prototypes
  • Iterative testing and refinement based on user trials

Technical Skills & Tools

This internship developed my skills in:

  • CAD design for assistive technology applications
  • Manual machining including milling and lathe operations
  • User-centered design and accessibility considerations
  • Prototype fabrication and testing methodologies
  • Cross-functional collaboration with clinical professionals
  • Design for manufacturing and assembly (DFMA)

Impact & Learnings

Working at AT Dev Inc taught me that the most rewarding engineering work directly improves people's lives. Seeing users interact with prototypes I helped create gave me a deeper appreciation for user-centered design and the importance of empathy in engineering.

I learned to balance technical constraints with real-world usability. A device might be mechanically sound, but if it's uncomfortable, difficult to put on, or doesn't fit into a user's daily routine, it won't be successful. This experience fundamentally shaped how I approach design problems—always starting with the user's needs.
